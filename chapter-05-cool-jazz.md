# 5. Cool Jazz & West Coast Jazz (Late 1940s-1950s)

### Historical Context

[*To be written: Cool jazz as a response to bebop's intensity; Birth of the Cool sessions 1949; West Coast scene in Los Angeles; White middle-class audience; Connection to classical music and Third Stream; GI Bill allowing musicians to study formally; California relaxed culture vs. New York intensity*]

### Relationship to Earlier Styles

[*To be written: Reaction to bebop's heat; draws on Lester Young's lighter approach; arranged sections returning from swing era; slower tempos; emphasis on composition vs. only improvisation*]

### Musical Characteristics

[*To be written: Lighter tones; arranged counterpoint; classical influences; modal thinking beginning; restrained emotion; emphasis on middle register; vibraphone and French horn added; piano less percussive*]

### Key Musicians

[*To be written: Miles Davis, Gerry Mulligan, Chet Baker, Dave Brubeck, Paul Desmond, Lennie Tristano, Lee Konitz, Stan Getz, Modern Jazz Quartet, Shorty Rogers*]

### Vocal Traditions

[*To be written: Chet Baker's intimate singing; Cool vocal styles; Connection to popular song*]

### Essential Recordings

[*To be written: Birth of the Cool (Miles Davis), Time Out (Brubeck), Getz/Gilberto, Chet Baker Sings, MJQ albums*]

### Cultural Impact

[*To be written: Intellectual appeal; college audiences; West Coast scene; Influence on film scores; Third Stream movement; Commercial success*]
